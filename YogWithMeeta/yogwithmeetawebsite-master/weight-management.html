The path to achieving something in life arises the moment the trigger points in your mind are activated. A human being’s mind and body is layered into different personalities that lead to alter their mind-set.

One of the most ancient conceptualizations of a human being’s personality comes from the theory of Pancha Kosha. The theory of Pancha Kosha was first described in the Taittiriya Upanishad. It describes that a human being is said to have five sheaths or layered personality.

The term ‘Pancha Kosha’ is derived from the Sanskrit word pancha, meaning ‘five’ and kosha, meaning ‘sheath’. The five sheaths of human soul consists:

  • Annamaya kosha – the food sheath: the physical body that requires food and nourishment to thrive.
  • Pranamaya kosha – the breath sheath: exists within the physical body and flows in the circulatory, lymphatic and nervous systems.
  • Manomaya kosha – the mind sheath: governs perception of the world and develops a sense of the Self.
  • Vijnanamaya kosha – the intellect sheath: the conscious layer that governs the sense of morals and ethics that are responsible for inner growth and gaining knowledge.
  • Anandamaya kosha – the bliss sheath: uniting all the koshas and experiencing unity with consciousness deeper than the materialistic world.
