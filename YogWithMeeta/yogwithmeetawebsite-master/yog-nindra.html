Yoga Nidra is a Sanskrit term meaning “yogic sleep” – often used as a deep relaxation technique and a form of meditation.

The ‘psychic sleep’ or Yoga Nidra is a technique where the practitioner’s body is in complete relaxation while also being in between a state of sleep and waking. In this state the practitioner turns their awareness inward following vocal instructions. Yoga Nidra involves the practice of pratyahara that is, withdrawal of senses. It results in deep relaxation and expansion of individual’s self-awareness.

Yoga Nidra was practiced by ancient sages to watch their impressions of mind, also known as samskaras. However, it was first mentioned in the Upanishads in association with Lord Krishna.

“[The Ocean] becomes the bed of the lotus-naveled Vishnu when at the termination of every Yuga that deity of immeasurable power enjoys Yoga-nidra, the deep sleep under the spell of spiritual meditation.”

~ Mahabharata, Book 1, section XXI

The meditation and conscious relaxation practice of Yoga Nidra cools down the mind and body and helps us return to our realest forms. It is an immensely powerful form of systematic meditation that offers various health benefits that include:

  • Relaxing and training the mind
  • Counteracting stress (mental and physical)
  • Soothes nervous system
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Clear cluttered thoughts and opens mind to learning, creativity and new material
  • Effective relief for depression and anxiety
  • Helps in coping with psychosomatic diseases such as tensions, conflicts and frustrations
  • Reduces fatigue
  • Soothes nervous system
  • Lowers high cholesterol and blood pressure level
  • Boosts immunity

Yoga Nidra alters an individual’s beliefs on the subconscious level. In the process of Yoga Nidra, the practitioner enters their alpha state thereby focusing on the third eye chakra – sahasrara. The practitioner hence looks beyond their bodily self and is able to clear up negative emotions as an aid to their physical well-being.

Practicing Yoga Nidra regularly helps keep our mind and body in harmony thereby promoting better mental and physical performance.
